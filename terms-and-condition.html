Terms and Conditions

Purpose of T&Cs

To protect the business by setting rules for using the service or product and clarifying the relationship between the business and its customers.

Key Elements

  • Only people who create an account and agree to the T&Cs might have access.
  • Users must not engage in harmful, illegal, or abusive behaviors, such as hacking, spreading malware, or harassing others.
  • Users can choose to deactivate or close their accounts through specific procedures, such as submitting a request or using a built-in option in the website's settings.
  • tates that the product or service is provided without guarantees or warranties of any kind, whether express or implied.
  • The business typically specifies that it is not liable for indirect, incidental, consequential, or punitive damages, such as loss of profits or data.
  • Clearly outline the types of subscriptions available (e.g., monthly, yearly, free trial) and what they include.
  • Specify whether users retain ownership of the content they create and share (e.g., text, images, videos).

Importance

Terms and Conditions are vital to protect both the business and the customer. They should be clear, transparent, and easy to understand.
